Pneumatic Folding Machine

A functional prototype for rapid, consistent shirt folding.

Project Overview: Mechanical Design and Sequential Control

This project successfully designed and fabricated a functional prototype for a pneumatic folding machine, managing the entire process from the initial concept phase to final assembly. The primary goal was to create a solution capable of reliable, automated sequencing for rapid and consistent shirt folding.

Implementation and Control

The system required the development and integration of diverse mechanical mechanisms to achieve complex, precise folding movements. Key components included a **pinion and rack system** and a **pulley and gear mechanism**.

The core control system was engineered from scratch, utilizing **pneumatic pistons** for actuation and **electrical relays** to manage the sequential timing and operation of all folding arms. This approach ensured the machine's control logic was robust and reliable for automated operation.

The project was a comprehensive demonstration of applied mechatronics, combining mechanical design, electrical control, and sequencing logic. The working prototype and its underlying engineering principles were successfully presented at the National Academic Engineering Conference.

Recognition

Result

The final prototype demonstrated reliable, automated sequencing, successfully folding shirts rapidly and consistently. The design proves proficiency in integrating mechanical systems with fundamental electrical control for industrial automation applications.

Technology Stack (Components Used)

  • Pneumatic Cylinders
  • Pneumatic Valves
  • Air Hoses
  • Electrical Relays
  • Arduino Microcontroller
  • Pinion and Rack System (Mechanism)
  • Pulley and Gear (Mechanism)
